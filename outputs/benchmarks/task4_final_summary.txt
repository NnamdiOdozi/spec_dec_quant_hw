# Task 4 final summary: vLLM serving benchmark comparison

Benchmark setup:
- Model family: Qwen/Qwen3-8B
- Dataset: philschmid/mt-bench
- Number of prompts: 80
- Maximum request concurrency: 8
- Endpoint: /v1/completions
- Benchmark tool: vllm bench serve

## Speedup versus baseline by output-token throughput

- EAGLE speculative: 1286.09 / 1128.40 = approximately 1.14x.
- FP8: 1618.27 / 1128.40 = approximately 1.43x.
- FP8 + EAGLE speculative: 1185.81 / 1128.40 = approximately 1.05x.

## Interpretation

FP8 was the best-performing configuration in this run. It improved output-token throughput from 1128.40 tok/s to 1618.27 tok/s, or about 1.43x baseline throughput. It also reduced mean TPOT from 7.01 ms to 4.87 ms and mean ITL from 7.01 ms to 4.87 ms.

EAGLE speculative decoding improved output-token throughput on the non-FP8 model from 1128.40 tok/s to 1286.09 tok/s, or about 1.14x. However, the acceptance rate was low at about 15%, with acceptance length around 1.46. Position-level acceptance fell quickly after the first drafted token.

FP8 + EAGLE speculative was successful, but it was slower than FP8 alone. This suggests that, for this trained drafter and benchmark setting, the speculative-decoding overhead outweighed the benefit when combined with FP8. The low acceptance rate is the likely explanation.
